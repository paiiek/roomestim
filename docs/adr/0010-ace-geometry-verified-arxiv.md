# ADR 0010 — ACE Challenge adapter geometry table verified against arXiv:1606.03365 Table 1 (dims only)

- **Status**: Accepted (v0.5.0)
- **Date**: 2026-05-07
- **Cross-ref**: D13, D14, ADR 0008, ADR 0009,
  `.omc/research/ace-table-i-acquisition.md`,
  `.omc/plans/v0.5-design.md` §0a,
  `.omc/plans/v0.4-audit-findings.md` Finding 1,
  `tests/test_ace_geometry_audit.py`,
  `tests/fixtures/ace_eaton_2016_table_i_arxiv.csv`,
  `docs/ace_geometry_audit_2026-05-07.md`.

## Decision

The dimensional half of the v0.4-DEFERRED Finding 1 audit lands at v0.5:

- All 7 rooms in `ACE_ROOM_GEOMETRY` (`roomestim/adapters/ace_challenge.py`)
  are verified byte-for-byte against arXiv:1606.03365 Table 1 (TASLP supporting
  material; open access; transcribed 2026-05-06) within ±0.01 m.
- `Office_2` is patched at v0.5.0: `W` 3.50 → 3.22 m, `H` 3.00 → 2.94 m. This
  is the only numerical correction in v0.5.0; previous values were a real
  numerical bug (V_m³ was 53.55 → now 48.28 — about a 10% shrink).
- Material assignments (`floor`, `walls`, `ceiling`) remain UNVERIFIED. The
  Eaton 2016 TASLP final paper is paywalled and the open arXiv preprint does
  not reproduce the materials; verification is DEFERRED to v0.6+ pending
  TASLP access.
- L/W convention: roomestim keeps "longer dimension as L" for adapter
  consistency. arXiv 1606.03365 Table 1 lists `Office_1`, `Office_2`, and
  `Building_Lobby` with the shorter dimension first. For these rooms the
  product (and therefore V_m³ and Sabine RT60) is identical regardless of
  L/W ordering; only `Office_2` carried a real numerical drift and is the
  only row patched.

## Drivers

- D13 v0.4 audit deferred Finding 1 explicitly because the local ACE corpus
  carries no per-room geometry/material breakdown.
- D14 reaffirmed F1 as DEFERRED at v0.4 with the explicit reverse-trigger
  "Eaton 2016 Table I becomes available and disagrees with `ACE_ROOM_GEOMETRY`".
- The parallel `cwm:websearchwithme` research at
  `.omc/research/ace-table-i-acquisition.md` surfaced arXiv:1606.03365 — the
  TASLP supporting material — as an open-access source for the dimensional
  half of Table 1. The materials half remained TASLP-locked.
- v0.5 SHORT-mode scope thus locks to "partial-A (dims-only) + B" per
  `.omc/plans/v0.5-design.md` §0a.

## Alternatives considered

- **Byte-faithful arXiv ordering for all rows.** Rejected. The arXiv L/W
  ordering swap on Office_1, Office_2, and Building_Lobby is product-equivalent
  (V_m³ identical) and would force a cosmetic 3-row diff with zero effect on
  Sabine/Eyring RT60. Larger churn for no numerical or correctness benefit.
- **Wait for TASLP final paper before any patch.** Rejected. The Office_2
  drift (W 3.50 → 3.22, H 3.00 → 2.94) is a real numerical bug that shifts
  Sabine RT60 at 500 Hz by ~10%; sitting on a known dimensional bug while
  waiting on a paywalled paper for the unrelated materials half is worse than
  shipping the dimensional fix today and DEFERRING materials.
- **Ship Scenario C (Millington-Sette per-surface predictor) instead.**
  Rejected. ADR 0009 already considered Millington-Sette as an alternative-
  considered. arXiv:1606.03365 supplied a real bug fix at v0.5 — that is the
  higher-value v0.5 headline. Millington-Sette stays a v0.6+ candidate.

## Why chosen

arXiv:1606.03365 is the canonical author-published supporting material for
Eaton 2016 TASLP, distributed under open access. Its Table 1 reproduces the
per-room dimensions used to compute the published RT60 figures. Treating it
as the source of truth for dims (with a declared "TASLP final not
cross-checked" caveat) is the cleanest path that respects D14 reverse-trigger
semantics: if the TASLP final paper later differs from the arXiv preprint,
the same audit infrastructure (committed CSV + gated test) re-runs and
flags any disagreement.

## Consequences

- (+) Office_2 dimensional drift fixed; Office_2 V_m³ shrinks 53.55 → 48.28
  (~10%) and Sabine RT60 prediction at 500 Hz drops correspondingly.
- (+) `tests/fixtures/ace_eaton_2016_table_i_arxiv.csv` is now a committed
  immutable provenance record; future re-runs of
  `tests/test_ace_geometry_audit.py` are deterministic.
- (+) `docs/ace_geometry_audit_2026-05-07.md` (auto-regenerated by the gated
  test) carries the per-room delta status and the materials-NOT-cross-checked
  caveat in the header so downstream readers see the limitation immediately.
- (+) `docs/perf_verification_e2e_2026-05-07.md` regenerates with the new
  Office_2 V_m³ when the gated E2E re-runs.
- (+) D14 reverse-trigger for the dims half resolves from DEFERRED → DONE.
  Materials and the Lecture_2 ceiling hypothesis (F3) and the
  `MaterialAbsorptionBands` coefficient revision (F4a) all stay DEFERRED.
- (+) d-inv-3 byte-equality of default-lane tests preserved: the ACE adapter
  is not imported in any default-lane test (the gated E2E and audit tests
  are both `@pytest.mark.e2e`); existing 80 tests remain byte-equal.
- (−) Materials remain unverified. Until TASLP access lands, an unknown
  fraction of the bare-walls error envelope on the high-error rooms
  (Building_Lobby, Lecture_1, Office_1, Office_2 over-predict at 500 Hz; see
  `docs/perf_verification_e2e_2026-05-07.md`) cannot be attributed to either
  material assignment or coefficient mis-specification. F3, F4a stay open.
- (−) The ±0.01 m tolerance accepts rounding noise from arXiv's 2-decimal
  publication precision (e.g., Office_2 listed as `(3.22, 5.1, 2.94)` —
  trailing zero on `5.10` is ours). This is acceptable for shoebox V_m³
  computation but should not be used to cross-check higher-precision sources
  if they later become available.

## Reverse if

- Eaton 2016 TASLP final paper (paywalled today) becomes available and its
  Table I numbers differ from arXiv:1606.03365 Table 1 → patch
  `ACE_ROOM_GEOMETRY`, regenerate the audit fixture, re-run the gated test.
- The materials cross-check (when TASLP access lands) disagrees with the
  current `floor` / `walls` / `ceiling` strings → patch the adapter and
  re-run the gated E2E perf doc.
- An external consumer reports the patched Office_2 V_m³ shrinks their
  workflow's behaviour materially → revert and re-open the audit.
