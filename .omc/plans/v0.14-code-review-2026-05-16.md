## §4 Verdict on the 3 executor-flagged STOP issues

### Concern #1: STOP rule #2 widening (analytic shoebox moderate-α ±5 % → ±15 %)

**Reviewer judgment**: ACCEPTABLE.

The widening is physically defensible: pure-specular ISM (Allen & Berkley) on a uniform-α cube produces a +8 to +12 % offset from Eyring at moderate absorption because Eyring's diffuse-field assumption (random reflection redistribution) breaks down at α=0.5 where specular reflections still dominate the EDC. The Lehmann-Johansson 2008 §III placement-bias analysis confirms this regime mismatch. The test docstring at `tests/test_image_source.py:131-184` is clear and explicit about the characterisation rationale; the assertion message at `:177-183` includes the `STOP rule #2 would re-fire if ratio departs from [0.85, 1.25]` regression-guard.

**ADR 0028 mention**: YES, ADR 0028 §Decision sub-item 2 lines 100-112 mention the widening explicitly, attributing it to "physical Eyring-Taylor-limit effect" + "investigation outcome branch of STOP rule #2". RELEASE_NOTES_v0.14.0.md:88-97 also documents it. Audit trail satisfactory.

**No reviewer-prescribed change**. The widening is `STOP rule #2 "acceptable executor action" branch` activated correctly.
